About Dog Licensing

In New York, it is required by law to license your dog. Licensing helps to ensure that dogs are properly vaccinated against rabies, which helps to protect both the dog and the community. It also helps to reunite lost dogs with their owners. To obtain a dog license in New York, you will need to provide proof that your dog has been vaccinated against rabies. The dog license fee varies depending on the location, but it is typically a one-time fee that is valid for the lifetime of the dog. Some areas may require annual renewal, so it's important to check with your local licensing authority. You can apply for a dog license at your local city or town clerk's office or online through the New York State Department of Agriculture and Markets website. You will need to provide information about your dog, including its name, breed, color, and age, as well as your contact information. It's important to note that if you move to a new area within New York, you will need to transfer your dog's license to your new location. This can typically be done by contacting your local licensing authority and providing them with your updated information.
